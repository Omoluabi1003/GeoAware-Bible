# GeoAware Bible Manifesto

GeoAware Bible is a reverent Scripture Experience Platform for pilgrims. Its product decisions, architecture, and visual design are governed by a simple conviction:

**Scripture first.**

## We Build for Pilgrims

GeoAware Bible is pilgrim-centered. It is designed for people seeking to walk through Scripture with attention, humility, and understanding.

The platform should feel calm, readable, and trustworthy. It should not pressure pilgrims to rush, perform, compare, or remain online longer than is spiritually or practically useful.

## We Reject Addictive Mechanics

GeoAware Bible must not use addictive mechanics. It must not optimize for compulsive engagement, streak anxiety, manipulative notifications, or endless feeds.

The purpose is not more screen time. The purpose is deeper encounter with Scripture.

## We Reject Feature Bloat

Every feature must earn its place by serving the mission. More features do not automatically create a better pilgrimage.

If a feature distracts from Scripture, complicates the experience, weakens performance, or exists only because other apps have it, it should not be added.

## We Do Not Imitate Social Media

GeoAware Bible is not a social-media product. It should not imitate social feeds, popularity metrics, vanity loops, or attention economies.

Community may be considered only when it supports reverence, care, and meaningful formation without turning Scripture into content for performance.

## Reverence Over Spectacle

Visual beauty matters, but reverence matters more. Living Earth, maps, animations, and interface details should support awe without becoming the object of attention.

The design language should be calm, minimal, timeless, and elegant.

## Clarity Over Complexity

GeoAware Bible should make hard things approachable without pretending they are simple. Clarity is not shallowness; it is disciplined design.

Interfaces, explanations, architecture, and naming should reduce confusion and help pilgrims continue walking.

## The Guiding Question

Before adding, changing, or promoting anything, ask:

> Does this help the pilgrim walk the Word?

If not, do not build it.
