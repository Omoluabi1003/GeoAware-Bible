# GeoAware Bible Founding Covenant

GeoAware Bible is founded by **Paul Iyogun**, **Founder and Chief Architect** of ETL GIS Consulting LLC, with a clear and lasting vision:

> To be remembered as the guy who used code to walk pilgrims through Scripture.

This covenant exists to protect the heart of the work before features, screens, maps, models, or releases begin to compete for attention.

## Mission

GeoAware Bible exists to help pilgrims walk through Scripture with greater awareness of place, language, history, and meaning. Its mission is not merely to present biblical content on a screen, but to guide people into a more thoughtful encounter with the Word.

The platform should help pilgrims understand that Scripture unfolded in real places, among real people, across real journeys. Every tool, map, reading interface, note, animation, and guide should serve that pilgrimage.

## Scripture-First Design

Scripture is the destination. Technology is only the road.

GeoAware Bible must always keep Scripture as the primary experience. Design should make the Word clearer, not busier. Architecture should make the experience more reliable, not more complicated. Visual systems should support attention, not capture it for their own sake.

When a product decision is unclear, the guiding question is:

> Does this help the pilgrim walk the Word?

If the answer is no, the decision should be reconsidered.

## Technology as Servant

GeoAware Bible may use modern technology, including maps, geospatial systems, language tools, offline experiences, and AI assistance. Yet technology must remain a servant, not the centerpiece.

AI should assist but never replace biblical study. Maps should reveal context rather than become distractions. Animations should be subtle and meaningful. Performance, accessibility, and clarity must come before visual spectacle.

## Closing Covenant

We commit this project to reverent craftsmanship, patient architecture, and Scripture-first design.

We will not build for noise, addiction, vanity, or feature bloat. We will build for pilgrims.

We will remember the founder's vision, preserve the mission, and use code as a road that helps people walk through Scripture.

**Walk the Word.**
