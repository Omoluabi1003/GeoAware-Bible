# GeoAware Bible Architecture

GeoAware Bible is a Scripture Experience Platform organized around pilgrimage through Scripture. The architecture should remain modular, purposeful, accessible, and performance-conscious.

## Architectural North Star

Scripture remains the primary experience. Technology exists to illuminate Scripture.

Each system should serve the question:

> Does this help the pilgrim walk the Word?

## Core Product Systems

### Living Earth

The Living Earth is the geospatial and visual context layer for Scripture. It should present biblical places with reverent, subtle, and meaningful visual design. Its purpose is context, not spectacle.

### Journey Engine

The Journey Engine organizes the pilgrim's movement through passages, places, themes, and milestones. It should support structured walking without turning the experience into gamification.

### Scripture Reader

The Scripture Reader is the central reading experience. It must prioritize readability, accessibility, performance, and respect for licensing. No copyrighted Bible text should be included without verified permission.

### GeoPrayer

GeoPrayer connects place-aware reflection with prayerful attention. It should encourage thoughtful response without performative sharing or manipulative engagement loops.

### Language Engine

The Language Engine supports multilingual access, translation awareness, original-language context where appropriate, and language-inclusive design. It should clarify rather than overwhelm.

### Bible Atlas

The Bible Atlas provides maps, places, routes, regions, and geographic references connected to Scripture. It should help pilgrims understand context while keeping the biblical text central.

### Pilgrim Journal

The Pilgrim Journal gives pilgrims a private, reflective space for notes, observations, prayers, and milestones. It should protect user trust and avoid social-media imitation.

### Scripture Passport

The Scripture Passport records meaningful milestones in a pilgrim's journey through Scripture. It should be commemorative rather than competitive and must avoid addictive mechanics.

### AI Guide

The AI Guide assists pilgrims with explanations, summaries, geographic context, language questions, and study prompts. It must never replace Scripture, pastoral wisdom, or serious biblical study.

### Offline Engine

The Offline Engine supports access where practical without network dependence. Offline-first design should be pursued when it improves reliability, accessibility, and usefulness for pilgrims.

## Quality Priorities

1. Scripture-first experience.
2. Accessibility as mandatory.
3. Performance before visual effects.
4. Maintainable architecture.
5. Subtle, meaningful motion.
6. Responsible use of AI and licensed content.
